Videos

I've been making content online for a pretty long time! Since then, I've accumulated a moderately large backlog of videos, most of which are on YouTube. Below, I've listed some of my favourites, my most ambitious, and my most successful.

If any of these videos spark your interest, consider supporting me on YouTube! I'm also open to editing commissions; shoot me an email if there's a project you'd like to start with me!



The Harbinger was an entry into gsgiraffes' "karate.gif" collab, where entrants were tasked with taking this GIF and creating something new from it. This "something" could be comical, story-driven, abstract, etc. I opted for all three of these. The result was an experiment in unconventional storytelling presented through preexisting media. I learned several new editing techniques and became familiar with a few new tools through this video.
Raito is one of many Mandelbrot Set zooms I've created in the past using Kalles Fraktaler. This one is likely the best in terms of quality; I had become quite familiar with the program at this point, both in terms of usage and customisation. While this video doesn't showcase my editing capabilities, it does demonstrate my comprehension of visual design and my ability to learn and use multiple pieces of software to create videos (and more generally, visual art).
1001410 & Square Adding in an investigation into a specific mathematical operator and its dynamics, particularly its relevance to the number 1001410. The video was made for James Grime's #MegaFavNumbers playlist, a collection of videos in a similar spirit. I aimed to present my findings in a narrative-driven way, allowing for anyone, regardless of mathematical background, to follow along and understand (or at least appreciate). I've certainly improved my video production in a number of ways since this video, but I still think it's a great demonstration of my ability to craft meaningful stories.
crushing things with a car gets you views is my most recent commentary-style video. These videos are some of my favourite to produce as they challenge me in a number of ways. The videos require a comprehensive review and critique of the content in question, an engaging narrative, and succinct editing. I'm also proud of the seamless live-action segments at the beginning and end, which feature an animated hexagon character edited in alongside me.
The BIOS Corruption Collab was a collaboration I hosted with several other content creators online. Participants showcased their unique "BIOS Corruptions", the results of taking video game console files and scrambling their code. The entries can range from pretty comical to downright eerie. This video was the result of a lot of dedication from me and everyone who joined, and I'm glad I was able to encourage so many people to try their hand at experimental art creation!
memories of mobius is a track from my 2020 EP "remains of Z". The music video was created using recontextualised clips from Mark Haynes' animation series Super Mario Bros. Z, where the EP's samples originate from. The music video exists in a limbo between existential dread and deliberate insincerity. It's not sure whether it wants to invoke bittersweet memories of one's childhood, or elicit laughter from its overdramatic portrayal of a Mario and Sonic fan animation. Maybe they're one of the same.
